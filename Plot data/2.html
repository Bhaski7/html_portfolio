Plot Summary

"Spider-Man" is a superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. Directed by Sam Raimi, the film follows Peter Parker (Tobey Maguire), a high school student who gains superpowers after being bitten by a genetically engineered spider. Peter develops enhanced strength, agility, and the ability to cling to surfaces and shoot webs from his wrists.

Initially using his powers for personal gain, Peter is devastated when his Uncle Ben (Cliff Robertson) is killed by a thief Peter had earlier allowed to escape. Remembering Uncle Ben's advice, "With great power comes great responsibility," Peter resolves to use his abilities to fight crime as Spider-Man.

Peter's dual life becomes complicated as he faces the Green Goblin (Willem Dafoe), the alter ego of industrialist Norman Osborn, whose son Harry (James Franco) is Peter's best friend. The Green Goblin terrorizes New York City, leading to several intense confrontations with Spider-Man. The conflict becomes personal when the Goblin kidnaps Mary Jane Watson (Kirsten Dunst), Peter's love interest.



In a final showdown, Peter defeats the Green Goblin, who inadvertently kills himself with his own weapon. Peter decides to keep his identity as Spider-Man a secret to protect his loved ones, setting the stage for future challenges and sacrifices.
